Learning Techniques

Kinesthetic

Kinesthetic learning is all about movement.Some techniques you may find useful are:

  • Journey method-Attach information, in order, to key points of a journey you know well . Repeat the journey in your mind regularly and also recall the information when you actually make the journey too
  • Story/song-Split the information into smaller pieces and turn these into pictures in your mind. Now, link these images using a song or story and combine the song/story with relevant actions
  • Writing-by making mind maps or index cards and copying out information
  • Games-Use online games do quizzes, tests and role play

Visual

Visual learning is all about looking at stuff.Some techniques you may find useful are:

  • Journey method-described in kinesthetic section
  • Memory hooks-Use images that look like the shape shape of the number (e.g. 1 looks like a pen). Split the information you need to revise into pieces, in order, or whatever type of connection works best for you and associate each of these pieces with the relevant memory hook
  • reading-You can make colourful posters, note pages or index cards. Lay out information in a way that it’s easy for you to revise from. You could also stick a list of facts you need to remember to a wall or somewhere you look regularly
  • Videos-Watch videos or documentaries

Auditory

Auditorial learning is all about listening. Some techniques you may find useful are:

  • Mnemonics-e.g.Big Elefants Can’t Always Use Small Exits can help you remember how to spell because
  • Memory hooks-Similar to the visual version but use images to rhyme with the number (e.g.four rhymes with door) instead of images that look like the number.
  • Quick fire-Write a list of questions and the answers to it. Get asked question 1, get told the answer, get asked question 2, get told the answer, get asked question 1 but this time you tell the answer. Repeat down the entire list, hearing a new question and answer, then following it by being asked the previous questions and answering them yourself.
  • Videos-Watch videos or documentaries
  • Story/songrefer to kinesthetic section
